Unit 1 Study Guide

A Christmas Carol

Central Theme(s) - The spirit of Christmas, Redeeming yourself for past transgressions, the idea of social injustice.

  • Scrooge - Mean, ill-spirited man who does not like Christmas and is very cheap. Treats his clerk, Cratchit poorly due to his cheapness. His nephew visits and invites him to a party, respond poorly, and says no. His former partner in the ghost-form visits him and wants him to the punishment for living the life that he is living. The ghost is bound to roam the earth with chains on him due to his behavior. Warns Scrooge of three ghosts that will visit him.

  • Ghosts - Ghost of Christmas Past Escorts Scrooge on a journey through his childhood’s Christmases. Reminisces on a friendship he had with Fezziwig, and his engagement to a girl named Belle who left him for his greed and money lust. Scrooge cries and gets taken back to bed.

  • Ghost of Christmas Present Giant clad in a green fur robe Takes Scrooge through London to show the Christmas festivities that will occur that year. Watches the family of his clerk, the Cratchits, as they enjoy their quaint Christmas meal in their small house. He sees Bob Cratchit's son, Tiny Tim, who happens to be crippled. He then witnesses the Christmas party that his nephew invited him to. Spirit is growing old, and then shows Scrooge two kids that are poor and starved Ignorance and Want.

  • Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come Shows Scrooge scenes of a death of someone whom he does not know the name of. Shows someone talking about the dead man’s riches, trading personal items for cash, and a couple that is thankful that he is dead. He is then taken to the dead man’s grave and sees his own name. He begs the spirit to change his fate.

  • His fate caused him to completely change how he has been. He gave a turkey to the Cratchit family, attends Fred’s Christmas party, donates to charities, treats Tiny Tim like his own kid, and most importantly, year after year, keeps the Christmas spirit alive.

To Build A Fire

Theme: Reveals the conflict arising between man’s fortitude and nature’s overwhelming power

  • The man enters the Yukon trail, looking for his companions.

  • “He was a new-comer in the land, a chechaquo, and this was his first winter.” (London 1)

    Foreshadowing his death by emphasizing his inexperience

  • Although he was told (by a elder man) traveling alone in such cold weather is dangerous, the man did not care and continued traveling

  • Man is unnamed and travels with his sole companion, a dog

    This technically shows the man's arrogance, as it relates back to him ignoring the old man's advice

  • As he travels on ice, he forces his dog to explore the area first to check if everything is safe

  • By noon, he sat down and decide to build a fire. However, he was shocked by how numb his hand and feet had become

  • The dog's instinct tells it to stop traveling and build a camp by the fire, but the man did not care

  • The man soon falls through ice into the water, freezing his lower body

  • The man knew he was in deep trouble and needed to build a fire immediately but failed after many attempts

  • After gathering bark and lighting a fire, snow from a tree above falls on it and snuffs the fire out

  • He then decides to kill the dog and warm his body with it's blood. (Fails due to his physical state)

  • After a last-ditch attempt at charging down the trail, the man dies without even making it 100ft down the trail

A White Heron

Theme: The call of nature can overpower human greed.

  • Hunter offers Sylvia $10 (lot of money at the time) if anyone can find the heron

  • Sylvia climbs up the tallest tree to find the heron, which she does

  • Sylvia does not divulge the secret of the heron in the end

  • Innocence and Nature vs Corruption of Man: Instead of giving into the temptation of money and greed presented by the hunter, Sylvia maintains her innocence and her respect toward nature by preserving the secret of the white heron.
