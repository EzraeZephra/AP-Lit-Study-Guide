Unit 1 Study Guide

A Christmas Carol

Central Theme(s) - The spirit of Christmas, redeeming yourself for past transgressions, the idea of social injustice.

    Scrooge - Mean, ill-spirited man who does not like Christmas and is very cheap.

    Treats his clerk, Cratchit poorly due to his cheapness.

    His nephew visits and invites him to a party, respond poorly, and says no.

    His former partner in the ghost-form visits him and wants him to the punishment for living the life that he is living

    The ghost is bound to roam the earth with chains on him due to his behavior

    Warns Scrooge of three ghosts that will visit him.

    --------

    Ghost of Christmas Past

    Glowing head

    Escorts Scrooge on a journey through his childhood’s Christmases.

    Reminisces on a friendship he had with Fezziwig, and his engagement to a girl named Belle who left him for his greed and money lust.

    Scrooge cries and gets taken back to bed.

    --------

    Ghost of Christmas Present

    Giant clad in a green fur robe

    Takes Scrooge through London to show the Christmas festivities that will occur that year.

    Watches the family of his clerk, the Cratchits, as they enjoy their quaint Christmas meal in their small house

    He sees Bob Cratchit's son, Tiny Tim, who happens to be crippled.

    He then witnesses the Christmas party that his nephew invited him to.

    Spirit is growing old, and then shows Scrooge two kids that are poor and starved Ignorance and Want.

    --------

    Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come

    Shows Scrooge scenes of a death of someone whom he does not know the name of.

    Shows someone talking about the dead man’s riches, trading personal items for cash, and a couple that is thankful that he is dead.

    He is then taken to the dead man’s grave and sees his own name.

    He begs the spirit to change his fate.

    His fate caused him to completely change how he has been.

    He gave a turkey to the Cratchit family, attends Fred’s Christmas party, donates to charities, treats Tiny Tim like his own kid, and most importantly, year after year, keeps the Christmas spirit alive.

To Build A Fire

Central Theme(s) - Reveals the conflict arising between man’s fortitude and nature’s overwhelming power

    The man enters the Yukon trail, looking for his companions.

    “He was a new-comer in the land, a chechaquo, and this was his first winter.” (London 1) (Foreshadows his death by emphasizing his inexperience)

    Although he was told (by a elder man) traveling alone in such cold weather is dangerous, the man did not care and continued traveling

    Man is unnamed and travels with his sole companion, a dog (This technically shows the man's arrogance, as it relates back to him ignoring the old man's advice)

    As he travels on ice, he forces his dog to explore the area first to check if everything is safe

    By noon, he sat down and decide to build a fire. However, he was shocked by how numb his hand and feet had become

    The dog's instinct tells it to stop traveling and build a camp by the fire, but the man did not care

    The man soon falls through ice into the water, freezing his lower body

    The man knew he was in deep trouble and needed to build a fire immediately but failed after many attempts

    After gathering bark and lighting a fire, snow from a tree above falls on it and snuffs the fire out

    He then decides to kill the dog and warm his body with it's blood. (Fails due to his physical state)

    After a last-ditch attempt at charging down the trail, the man dies without even making it 100ft down the trail

The Fish

Central Theme(s) - Beauty and inticacy of nature, do not judge a book by its cover (fish has had a long arduos life)

    Age References:

    The fish didn't fight, symbolizing that it was past its prime

    Skin described as "ancient wallpaper" (imagery of like wrinkly old skin / papyrus)

    Speckled with barnacles (like an old ship)

    Infested with tiny white sea-lice (maybe something like a rotten corpse?)

    Eyes are yellowed and akin to "old scratched isinglass" (isinglass is fish gelatin, its yellowish and see-through, like hardened honey)

    Sullen face builds upon its skin being like "ancient wallpaper"

    5 old pieces of fish-line on his lip + "like medals with their ribbons" (This combined with the frayed lines makes the fish seem like a decorated and hardened war veteran)

    "Five-haired beard of wisdom (Also describes the lines, but pictures the fish as a magical sage)

    --------

    Other Significant Imagery:

    Images of the narrator imagining the fish being gutted

    Oil being described as a rainbow (May show how the speaker is overcome with emotion and awe / or the narrator is at peace with the fish / or how this incident shines through the filthy bilge water like a rainbow )

A White Heron

Central Theme(s) - The call of nature can overpower human greed.

    Sylvia is a shy 9-year-old who is known to be in-tune with nature (She has no friends)

    Hunter offers Sylvia $10 (lot of money at the time) if she tells him where the heron is

    After spending some time with Hunter, she becomes attracted to him, though repelled by his bird hunting.

    Sylvia climbs up the tallest tree to find the heron

    She finds the Heron, but is enlightened with a "mystical union with nature"

    Sylvia does not divulge the secret of the heron in the end as she is not ready to surrender her relationship with nature for human interests

    Innocence and Nature vs Corruption of Man: Instead of giving into the temptation of money and greed presented by the hunter, Sylvia maintains her innocence and her respect toward nature by preserving the secret of the white heron.

Heart of Darkness

Central Theme(s) - The hypocrisy of imperialism and the evil and darkness brought along with it

    Part 1:

    Story is set on the Nellie, which is anchored at the mouth of the River Thames

    Five men on the ship: the director / captain, the lawyer, the accountant, Marlow, and the narrator

    Marlows travels to what is presumably Brussels (described as a whited sepulchre (like a tomb)) to sign his employment contract

    Marlow tells a story (one much after his journey to the Congo) on how Fresleven, his predecessor, was killed over some hens.

    The doctor takes measurements of Marlow's skull, in hopes of learning something that may give Belgians an advantage.

    Aunt bids Marlow farewell (Conrad was never able to IRL), and is ignorant that the company's true goal is not in civilization, but profit

    After getting on the French steamer to Africa, Marlow encounters a ship "firing into the continent".

    Arrives at the Company's outer station, which is super run-down

    Marlow remarks that he had known “The flabby, pretending, weak-eyed devil of a rapacious and pitiless folly” for his first impression of the outer station. (means that Marlow suggests the fundamental evil of imperialism is greed, not violence)

    Notices a group of dying native laborers, and offers a biscuit to one of them.

    Marlow is told about Kurtz and his great feats, and entrusts Marlow to update Kurtz on the situation at the outer station.

    Then travels in a caravan for 15 days to the central station

    One of his white companions falls ill and must be carried

    The steamer Marlow was supposed to command was sunk, who suspects that this is intentional

    Manager says that he took the boat out in a hurry to aid the inner stations, as Kurtz is rumored to be ill (sings Kurtz praises again)

    Marlow stays at the central station for 3 months to fix the ship

    --------

    Part 2:

    Kurtz noted to be ill in conversation between the manager and his uncle

    Preparation for the trip to find Kurtz, manager and pilgrims attend; assisted by the cannibals

    Banging of drums heard, reminiscent of church bells

    Journey to find Kurtz, old book by Russian trader found (book is in Russian)

    Fog rolls in and cry from the natives are heard; classified by Marlow as more sorrowful than warlike

    Cannibals hungry and are out of food (their rotten hippo meat was thrown out by the pilgrims); Marlow notes their incredible resistant in holding back their hunger; leader of the cannibal wants to eat the natives that cried out to them

    Ambush of arrows come from the foliage, pilgrims retaliate with guns; Marlow’s helmsman is impaled with a spear and dies on Marlow’s feet, he then throws off his blood-stained shoes

    Marlow extremely dissapointed as the crew believe that Kurtz is dead

    Women are "out of it" as in they are in their own fantasy land separate from the grim realities of the world; helps mens' world as women depict what the ideal society would be in their lives

    “Suppression of Savage Customs” notes as eloquent and powerful and noble as well; scribbled writings of “exterminate all the brutes” written by Kurtz as a later date (Irony is used here by Conrad through Kurtz unexpectedly saying to kill the brutes when he was preaching about helping them)

    Helmsmans body thrown overboard so that the cannibals will not eat it; Marlow noted to have some bond with him

    Meeting with the Russian trader who relays a another depiction of Kurtz, stating that he “enlarged his mind”

    --------

    Part 3:

    After arriving at Kurtz's place, Marlow talks with the Russian about Kurtz (The Russian is infatuated with Kurtz

    Kurtz is very ill and is dying during this period.

    Marlow discovers that there are heads on the fencepost

    Kurtz recognizes Marlow as he read about him in a letter.

    The manager goes to speak with Kurtz, and then Marlow sees Kurtz’s mistress who is described as having a strong hold on Kurtz.

    He manager comes out and talks to Marlow, explaining that Kurtz is unsound and closed off to the company for the time being (Marlow defends him and falls out of favor)

    Russian trader finds out that Kurtz ordered the attack on the steamer so that the manager would think he is dead and turn back (Ends up being threatened, and leaves)

    Marlow hears a loud scream, leaves the boat, checks Kurtz’s cabin, and notices that he is not there

    He decides to look for Kurtz and finds him crawling away on a dirt trail

    He sees that there are native rituals and informs Kurtz that he will be lost if he continues on with it (Kurtz returns to the boat)

    There is a crowd that watches him leave and they are filled with joy (Pilgrims open fire on this crowd)

    Manager ignores Marlow on the ride back to civilization because he too is unsound.

    Kurtz entrusts Marlow with his legacy, and gives him all his documents

    Kurtz dies saying "the horror!! the horror!!" (Could be seen as Kurtz reflecting on his time in the jungle?)

    After barely surviving his illnesses, Marlow returns to sepulchral city, Brussels

    Representative of the company comes and tries to retrieve the Kurtz's papers, but Marlow only gives them a cut version of his pamphlet on savage customs

    Another man who claims to be Kurtz's cousin comes and discussed Kurtz with Marlow, talks about Kurtz's other talents (music etc.)

    A journalist colleague of Kurtz takes the pamphlet given to Marlow for publication, stating that Kurtz's real skills were in extremist politics

    He visits Kurtz's fiancé and lies to her that his last words were her name.

    She praises Kurtz before and after hearing this.
