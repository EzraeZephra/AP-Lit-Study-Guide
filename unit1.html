To Build A Fire

Theme: Reveals the conflict arising between man’s fortitude and nature’s overwhelming power

  • The man enters the Yukon trail, looking for his companions.

  • “He was a new-comer in the land, a chechaquo, and this was his first winter.” (London 1)

    Foreshadowing his death by emphasizing his inexperience

  • Although he was told (by a elder man) traveling alone in such cold weather is dangerous, the man did not care and continued traveling

  • Man is unnamed and travels with his sole companion, a dog

    This technically shows the man's arrogance, as it relates back to him ignoring the old man's advice

  • As he travels on ice, he forces his dog to explore the area first to check if everything is safe

  • By noon, he sat down and decide to build a fire. However, he was shocked by how numb his hand and feet had become

  • The dog's instinct tells it to stop traveling and build a camp by the fire, but the man did not care

  • The man soon falls through ice into the water, freezing his lower body

  • The man knew he was in deep trouble and needed to build a fire immediately but failed after many attempts

  • After gathering bark and lighting a fire, snow from a tree above falls on it and snuffs the fire out

  • He then decides to kill the dog and warm his body with it's blood. (Fails due to his physical state)

  • After a last-ditch attempt at charging down the trail, the man dies without even making it 100ft down the trail

A White Heron

Theme: The call of nature can overpower human greed.

  • Hunter offers Sylvia $10 (lot of money at the time) if anyone can find the heron

  • Sylvia climbs up the tallest tree to find the heron, which she does

  • Sylvia does not divulge the secret of the heron in the end

  • Innocence and Nature vs Corruption of Man: Instead of giving into the temptation of money and greed presented by the hunter, Sylvia maintains her innocence and her respect toward nature by preserving the secret of the white heron.
